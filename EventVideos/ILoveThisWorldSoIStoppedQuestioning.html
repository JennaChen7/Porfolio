POEM
I Love This World, So I Stopped Questioning
Jenna Chen
September 11, 2020
"I fear that if I go down the road,
Continue questioning,
I would drown myself in confusion. "


For those who are struggling and confused


There are things in this world that I can never figure out.
The big questions.
Who live, who die.
Are we born with the curse of being evil?
Are we too selfish to do anything that only benefit others?
Are the great rulers wrong,
When children starve to death in warfares
Because they declare a justice war
Against the evil
And to help the oppressed?

I never had been down on my luck to have to experience such things.
So, I think, think,
And think.
Until I give myself a shabby answer,
Ridiculous and unconvincing.
Then I let go.

Cos

I fear that if I go down the road,
Continue thinking,
I would love this world less.

I fear that if I go down the road,
Continue questioning,
I would drown myself in confusion.

I fear that if I go down the road,
Continue challenging,
I would misinterpret the Facts
Which I only hear from others,
Which I only read from books,
Which I do not know the whole.

Iâ€™m not qualified
To give a thought,
To judge.
So I let go.

When I stop questioning,
It seems a lot easier to admit that I am
Coward,
Innocent,
And ordinary.
And most importantly,
After I got a taste of
The good first,
The bad after,
Then the good again,
My heart is still warm and
I think I love this world.
